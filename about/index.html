Q: What's with the old-timey site, gramps?

Revolutionary new technologies, and especially media technologies, simultaneously possess an empancipatory as well as oppressive potentiality. The Yippies, for example, saw television as an exciting medium to subvert hegemony; the '60s radicals were tantalised by the idea of hijacking mass media, using it as a tool for liberation. Of course -- as theorists were well aware -- these items have become some of the sharpest instruments of control.

The internet is the greatest media technology so far. In light of that, this web site is currently kept "back to basics" because its goal isn't to sell you ad-space or waste your time with bloat: decentralised publishing is the secret origin of today's zombie-net.
